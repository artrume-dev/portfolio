Category 1 · Reactive — Input & Intent Engine

How the AI listens and acts

These elements handle everything from the moment a customer types a message to the point where the AI knows exactly what action to take.

Critical
Pr
Prompts
Brand Voice Instructions
The written instructions you give the AI before every conversation. Like a briefing document — it tells the AI your brand's tone, what products to focus on, and how to format answers. Without this, the AI has no idea it's working for your brand.
Critical
Fc
Function Call
Smart Action Router
When the AI needs to do something (look up stock levels, add to cart, check pricing) it uses Function Calls to trigger the right action automatically. Think of it as the AI pressing buttons in your systems on behalf of the customer.
Core
Ag
Agent
Multi-Step Task Handler
An Agent can carry out a sequence of steps to complete a goal — search, then compare, then recommend, then add to cart. It's like a capable employee who doesn't need hand-holding for every step.
Phase 3
Ma
Multi-Agent
Parallel Research Team
Multiple AI agents working simultaneously on different parts of a query. Imagine a research team — one person checks outdoor gear, another checks footwear, all at the same time. Faster, more thorough answers for complex requests.

Category 2 · Retrieval — Product Knowledge Store

How the AI finds the right products

These elements turn your product catalog into something an AI can search intelligently — finding the right match even when a customer doesn't use the exact product name.

Critical
Em
Embeddings
Meaning Converter

Every product in your catalog (name, description, specs, reviews) is converted into a unique mathematical "fingerprint" that captures its meaning. "Waterproof hiking boots" and "trail walking shoes for wet weather" would have similar fingerprints, even though the words are different.

📖 Analogy
Like translating every product into a universal language that the AI can compare and understand — even if customers describe things in unusual ways.
Critical
Vx
Vector DB
Intelligent Product Library

This is the database where all those "fingerprints" are stored. When a customer asks a question, the Vector DB instantly finds the products whose fingerprints are closest to the question — returning the best matches in milliseconds, across tens of thousands of products.

📖 Analogy
Like a super-smart library card catalog — instead of searching by exact title, it searches by meaning and relevance.

Category 3 · Orchestration — Pipeline & Framework

How everything is coordinated

These elements act as the "operations manager" of the AI system — making sure each component runs in the right order and that responses are always grounded in your real product data.

Critical
Rg
RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation

RAG is the core process that stops the AI from making things up. Before answering, it first searches your product database for relevant information, then feeds that real data to the AI so it can compose an accurate, grounded response.

📖 Analogy
Like a journalist who looks up verified facts before writing a story — rather than guessing from memory.
Core
Fw
Framework
Orchestration Engine

The software framework (LangChain, LlamaIndex) connects all the pieces — managing conversation memory, retrieval chains, API calls, and ensuring the whole pipeline flows correctly from query to answer.

📖 Analogy
Like a kitchen pass in a restaurant — the chef (AI) receives orders, the pass coordinates who does what and in which order, making the whole experience seamless.

Category 4 · Validation — Safety & Trust Layer

How the AI stays safe and on-brand

Before any response reaches a customer, it passes through a safety and quality layer. These elements protect your brand, your customers, and your business from AI mistakes.

Critical
Gr
Guardrails
Response Safety Filters
Automated filters that catch and block problematic responses before they reach customers. Prevents hallucinated pricing, off-brand messaging, and harmful outputs. Think of them as the proofreader that runs before every reply is sent.
Core
Rt
Red-Teaming
Adversarial Stress-Testing
Before launch, a team (or automated tool) deliberately tries to break the AI — asking trick questions, edge cases, and provocative queries to find weak spots. Like a fire drill: you'd rather discover problems in training than in front of real customers.
Phase 2
In
Interpretability
AI Decision Explainer
Tools that let your team see why the AI made a specific recommendation. Essential for regulated industries (pharma, finance) where the brand must be able to account for every suggestion. Turns the AI from a "black box" into an auditable decision trail.

Category 5 · Models — AI Brain Selection

Choosing the right AI for the job

Not every task needs the most powerful (and expensive) AI. This category is about using the right model for each situation — balancing speed, cost, and capability.

Critical
Lg
LLM
Core Language Model
The main AI brain — models like Claude or GPT-4o. Handles understanding the customer's question and writing a natural, helpful response. The "writer and thinker" at the heart of the system. Tools: Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o, Gemini.
Phase 2
Mm
Multimodal
Image Understanding
AI that understands images as well as text. Enables visual product search: "find something like this photo." A customer can photograph an item they like and the AI finds similar products in your catalog.
Phase 2
Sm
Small Model
Fast Pre-Filter AI
A lightweight, low-cost AI that handles simple tasks quickly — like classifying what a customer wants before routing to the main model. Like a receptionist who takes your name before you see the specialist — saves time and money.
Phase 3
Th
Thinking Model
Deep Reasoning AI
Advanced models that "think through" complex problems step-by-step before answering. Used for comparing products across 50+ attributes, or handling high-consideration purchases where getting it exactly right matters more than speed.

System Architecture

How the five layers work together

A customer's question flows through these five layers in sequence — from raw text input to a safe, grounded, on-brand product recommendation.

1
C1 · Reactive
Input & Intent Engine
Customer types a question → Brand prompts define scope and tone → Function calls identify what action is needed → Agent decides how to proceed. Example: "Find waterproof hiking boots under £200" → routed to product search pipeline.
2
C2 · Retrieval
Product Knowledge Store
The question's meaning is encoded into a fingerprint (Embeddings) → the Vector DB finds the most semantically similar products in your catalog — regardless of exact keyword matches. Returns top matching products in milliseconds.
3
C3 · Orchestration
RAG Pipeline & Framework
The retrieved product data is injected into the AI's context (RAG) → the Framework manages memory, sequencing, and API calls → AI composes an answer grounded entirely in real product data — not guesswork.
4
C4 · Validation
Safety & Trust Layer
Guardrails check the response for hallucinated prices, off-brand language, or harmful content → blocked before it reaches the customer. Red-teaming and Interpretability tools protect the brand and enable accountability.
5
C5 · Models
Model Selection Strategy
The right AI model is selected for each task type — Fast Small Model for simple classification, LLM for language generation, Multimodal for image queries, Thinking Model for complex comparisons — balancing speed and cost.
